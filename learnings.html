Cassandra
Query-first · Compaction · Tombstones

Model for reads; pay for mistakes later.

Cassandra rewards predictable access paths and punishes flexible queries with hotspots, tombstones, and slow repairs.

Caching
Staleness · Stampedes · Invalidation

Cache correctness is a spectrum.

Choose bounded staleness and predictable failure modes over perfect invalidation that becomes operational debt.

Pipelines
Retries · Idempotency · Backpressure

Async systems need replay safety.

Retries happen. Make handlers idempotent, encode dedupe, and expose backpressure before latency becomes an outage.

Reliability
SLOs · Error budgets · Triage

SLOs prevent alert-driven engineering.

Define what matters to users, then pick signals that explain failures. Everything else becomes noise and burnout.

Kafka

Partitioning is a product decision

Partitioning determines what you can guarantee (ordering), what you can scale (parallel consumers), and where you will feel pain (hot keys, replays, migrations).

Cassandra

Model for reads; pay for mistakes later

Cassandra is excellent when your queries are known and stable. It is unforgiving when you need ad-hoc filtering or when partition strategy creates hotspots.

Caching

Cache correctness is a spectrum

Perfect invalidation is rarely worth it. Mature systems choose bounded staleness with explicit failure modes and guardrails against stampedes.

Async pipelines

Async systems need replay safety

Once you introduce queues and retries, duplicate processing is normal. The goal is making duplicates safe and observable.

Reliability

SLOs prevent alert-driven engineering

Define user-visible success, map alerts to actions, and use error budgets to allocate engineering time intentionally.

Data consistency

Consistency is a workflow, not a toggle

Distributed systems drift. Design for detection and repair with invariants, audits, and reconciliation loops.
